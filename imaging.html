Most Critical Change

ALL imaging Fastpasses have been eliminated. Every MRI, CT scan, and PET scan now requires full prior authorization — even from previously trusted, high-performing providers. Clinically urgent authorizations expire in just 3 days with zero extensions allowed.

🔴 The 11 Most Problematic Changes (Timestamped)

TimeWhat ChangedThe Problem
[10:36]🔴 All Imaging Fastpasses EliminatedEvery MRI, CT, PET scan now requires full PA — even from previously trusted high-performing providers. "Redesigned performance-based version" promised but no implementation date given.
[11:23]🔴 Non-Cardiology Providers Face Full Review for Cardiac StudiesPCPs, internists, ER doctors ordering echos or cardiac imaging now face full clinical review. Cardiologists retain real-time approval — discriminatory treatment without clinical justification.
[14:01]🔴 Proprietary Algorithm as Primary GatekeeperSecret algorithm makes first determination. No transparency, no accountability. For time-sensitive imaging (cancer, stroke, cardiac), algorithm delays can change clinical outcomes.
[14:52]🔴 Multi-Stage Information Loop Before DenialTwo sequential rounds of info requests before denial. Each round adds business days. For suspected malignancy or cardiac ischemia, 3-5 day delays change outcomes.
[8:03]🔴 Peer-to-Peer is Consultative OnlyCalling Evolent does not reverse denial. Consultative only — denial path locked once algorithm and reviewer decide. Same limitation as chiropractic program.
[9:08]🔴 Medicare Patients Have No Post-Denial PathNo recourse once denial issued. Providers must act on peer-to-peer BEFORE denial — window easily missed. Seniors most at risk; only recourse is longer HMSA formal appeal.
[6:17]🔴 Urgent Auth = 3 Days Validity, NO ExtensionsMost time-sensitive cases get shortest window with zero flexibility. If facility can't schedule within 3 days, authorization expires and provider must restart entire process.
[7:11]🔴 Secondary Insurance Requires Auth Even When HMSA is SecondaryMedicare Advantage + HMSA secondary = double authorization for single imaging study. Even after Medicare authorizes and pays, HMSA still requires separate Evolent auth.
[11:54]🟢 Code Descoping (Positive Change)Some CPT codes removed from PA requirement — genuine burden reduction. But specific codes NOT disclosed in training; providers must check utilization matrices manually.
[38:05]🔴 Rendering Provider Must Be in System or Auth Cannot ProceedIf imaging facility not in Evolent's RADMD system, provider cannot submit auth online — must call. Disadvantages independent, small, and rural facilities while favoring large health systems.
[37:24]🔴 Cardiac/Radiology Combination Studies ProhibitedPatient needing both cardiac and non-cardiac imaging requires TWO separate auth requests — each with own documentation, review cycle, and delays. Administrative decision, not clinical.
